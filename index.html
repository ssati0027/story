01 — The Beginning

I wasn't supposed to be a software engineer.

If you had met me in 2013, you would have found a student at STJP in Harapanahalli, bent over mechanical drawings and studying engineering principles in steel and metal. My world was physical — gears, tolerances, and load calculations. Software felt like a different universe entirely.

But somewhere along the way, something shifted. I found myself drawn not to the machines themselves, but to the logic behind them. The systematic thinking. The way a well-designed system could handle enormous complexity with graceful efficiency. That instinct didn't belong on a workshop floor — it belonged in code.

"The best engineers aren't always the ones who followed the straightest path. Sometimes the detour is exactly what gives you a unique perspective."

Making the switch wasn't easy or conventional. But looking back, every step — including the detour through mechanical engineering — shaped the way I think about systems today.

02 — First Steps

Writing my first API and falling in love with backend.

My professional journey started at Enhancesys Innovation Pvt. Ltd. in mid-2021, where I joined as a Graduate Trainee. It was a humble beginning — but it was mine. I built RESTful APIs with Spring Boot, wrestled with exception handling, and learned what it actually means to write code that other people and systems depend on.

Even reducing backend response time by 15% felt like a victory. More importantly, I discovered something important about myself: I genuinely loved this. The puzzle of making systems faster, more reliable, and more elegant — it energised me in a way I hadn't expected.

Six months in, I had a foundation. More crucially, I had direction.

03 — Finding My Footing

Where things got serious: MarkLogic, pipelines, and real scale.

In January 2022, I joined ITOrizon Pvt. Ltd. as a Software Analyst. This is where the real education began. The problems were bigger, the stakes were higher, and the data volumes were unlike anything I'd touched before.

I built MarkLogic Optic pipelines that prepared machine-learning-ready datasets from millions of documents. The challenge wasn't just getting the data right — it was getting it right fast. By shifting to row-based Optic analytics and optimizing index-backed queries, I cut AIML data preparation latency by 50%. Half the waiting time. Double the throughput.

That kind of result taught me something important: performance isn't a feature you bolt on later. It's a discipline you build into every decision, from schema design to query patterns to how you think about data at rest versus data in motion.

04 — Levelling Up

Senior, and what that word really means.

In January 2024, I stepped into the role of Senior Software Analyst at ITOrizon. The title change was less exciting to me than what came with it: ownership. I now owned Java backend services that process over 10 million records per day. I designed and shipped over 25 RESTful API endpoints handling more than 1,000 requests per second — with a p95 latency under 200 milliseconds.

But senior engineering, I learned, is about more than technical output. It's about multiplying the team. I led the refactoring of legacy backend modules — not just to make the code cleaner, but to make the codebase more welcoming. New engineers could onboard 20% faster because the system finally made sense. That felt as meaningful as any performance metric.

"The code you write is for the machine. The architecture you design is for the team that comes after you."

I also implemented a dynamic request routing and query generation framework that reduced code duplication by 40%, turning what had been a tangle of bespoke logic into a clean, configuration-driven workflow. Production defects dropped by 25% as I partnered closely with QA, frontend, and DevOps teams to raise the bar on code quality. And when production issues hit — memory leaks, thread starvation, service timeouts — I dug in and fixed them, pushing system uptime from 99.5% to 99.9%.

05 — My Toolkit

What I bring to a team.

Over four years of focused backend work, I've built a stack that I trust — and that I continue to deepen. I work primarily in Java, with Spring Boot and Spring Data JPA as my daily frameworks. On the data side, I've developed deep expertise in MarkLogic and SQL. For infrastructure, I've worked with AWS and S3 to build scalable, cloud-friendly services.

But beyond tools: I bring a production-first mindset. I've debugged live systems under pressure, thought carefully about concurrency and performance, and built APIs that other teams actually depend on. That responsibility sharpens you in ways that side projects simply cannot.
